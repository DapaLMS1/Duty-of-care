The Legal Obligation: Duty of Care

The Duty of Care is a legal obligation that requires dental assistants to adhere to a reasonable standard of care to prevent foreseeable harm to a patient. Click on a card to reveal its definition.

Establishment

A duty of care is automatically established once a patient-healthcare provider relationship begins (e.g., when a patient attends the practice). This immediate link is the foundation of legal accountability.

Standard of Care

The expected standard is that of a reasonably competent dental assistant operating in the same circumstances. This means your actions are measured against what a typical, well-trained professional would do.

Breach of Duty

A breach occurs when an assistant fails to meet this standard, and that failure results in a predictable injury or harm to the patient.

Examples of Potential Breach:
  • Failing to follow proper sterilisation procedures (leading to infection).
  • Not monitoring a patient experiencing distress.
  • Incorrect handling of sharps.

Negligence

Negligence is a breach of the duty of care that legally results in harm. Dental assistants can be held personally responsible for negligence in certain circumstances, highlighting the importance of strict adherence to protocols.
